About Extremism

Extremism refers to holding extreme political, religious, or ideological beliefs that reject social norms and can lead to harmful actions. It poses a global threat, affecting communities worldwide.

Types: Political, religious, and ideological extremism.

Causes: Social, economic, and psychological factors contribute to radicalization.

Recognizing Extremism

Warning signs of extremism include:

  • Isolation from friends and family.
  • Drastic behavioral or ideological changes.
  • Exposure to extremist content online.
  • Use of hate speech or promoting violence.

Regulate Extremism

Steps to regulate extremism:

  • Encourage open dialogue and inclusivity.
  • Educate communities about extremism and its risks.
  • Monitor and counter online radicalization.
  • Support at-risk individuals with guidance and resources.
